## Exam Tips

Advice from people who passed.

### Before the Exam

1. **Study the FINRA content outline** - Know exactly what's covered
2. **Take the official FINRA practice test** - It's the closest to the real thing
3. **Focus on the 4 knowledge areas:**
   - Knowledge of Capital Markets (16%)
   - Understanding Products and Their Risks (44%)
   - Understanding Trading, Customer Accounts and Prohibited Activities (31%)
   - Overview of Regulatory Framework (9%)

### During the Exam

- **75 questions, 1 hour 45 minutes** = ~1.4 minutes per question
- **10 unscored pilot questions** - Don't stress if some seem unusual
- **Passing score: 70%** - You need 53/75 scored questions correct
- **Flag and return** - Don't spend too long on hard questions

### Common Topics

These appear frequently:
- Types of securities (stocks, bonds, options, mutual funds)
- Regulatory bodies (SEC, FINRA, MSRB, state regulators)
- Customer account types and suitability
- Prohibited practices (insider trading, churning, front-running)
- Economic factors and interest rates

| Fact | Detail |
|------|--------|

| **Time** | 1 hour 45 minutes |
| **Passing Score** | 70% (53/75) |
| **Cost** | $80 |
| **Validity** | 4 years to associate with a firm |
| **Retake Wait** | 30 days (after 3 failures: 180 days) |
